How does the Irish voting system work?

Published on: 22nd March 2018

Ireland uses PR-STV (Proportional Representation with a Single Transferable Vote). This article is about the voting system for Local Elections, European Elections, General Elections, Presidential Elections and By-Elections. This article describes general elections however it applies to all other elections.

How do I fill out the ballot paper?

In your constituency 3-5 TD's will be elected. When filling out the ballot paper, put a 1 beside your favourite candidate. Put a 2 beside your second favourite candidate. Put a 3 beside your third favourite candidate and so on and so on. You do not have to give a preference to every candidate. You can put a preference for 1 candidate, all candidates or any number of candidates. You are basically saying to give your vote to the candidate you put the number 1 beside. Then if they get eliminated or elected with a surplus your vote will be given to your 2nd favourite candidate. If they get eliminated or elected with a surplus your vote goes to your 3rd favourite candidate and so on and so on. TIP: If there is a candidate you really, really don't want to be elected, fill out a preference for all canidates except for them.
